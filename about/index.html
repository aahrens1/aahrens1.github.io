I am an Assistant Professor at CERGE-EI, Prague. I am also Co-founder of Path2Work, Research Associate at the University of Lausanne, and Research Affiliate at the Immigration Policy Lab (Stanford/ETH Zurich). From 2019 to 2025, I was Senior Researcher at the Public Policy Group, ETH Zurich. Before that, I was at the Economic and Social Research Institute, Dublin. I hold an MSc in Economics from the University of Edinburgh and a PhD in Economics from Heriot-Watt University, Edinburgh.

Research

I am interested in applying causal inference and data science to policy-relevant questions. My empirical research focuses on migration and labour economics. I also have a strong interest in methodological questions, especially relating to the use of machine learning for causal inference. My research has been published in peer-reviewed journals such as Journal of Applied Econometrics, World Development, and Economics Letters.

Policy

I have contributed to policy projects for governmental organisations in the UK, Ireland and Switzerland. In Switzerland, I have evaluated the job vacancy notice obligation introduced in 2020, co-authored a report on algorithmic job recommendations, and co-founded Path2Work, which is an online platform for job matching targeted at refugees in Switzerland.
